7 PLASTIC TYPES

.

Plastic #1

Polyethylene terephthalate, or PETE, makes up most disposable beverage and food containers. Be careful not to heat up this plastic and avoid reusing it as a container. PETE products can be recycled.

Examples: Plastic bottles (water, soft drinks, cooking oil)

.

Plastic #2

High-Density Polyethylene, or HDPE, is a stiff plastic used to make milk jugs, toys, shampoo bottles, and more. It is safer than PETE and can be reused and recycled.

Examples: Milk jugs, cleaning agents, shampoo bottles, bleach bottles

.

Plastic #3

PVC, or Polyvinyl Chloride, is a soft, flexible plastic. PVC contains numerous toxins, and thus should not be reused and cannot be recycled.

Examples: Plastic food wrap, plastic piping, children's and pets' toys

.

Plastic #4

LDPE, or Low-Density Polyethylene, is a cheap, low cost plastic used to make many single use plastic bags. It cannot be recycled, but plastic bags can be reused.

Examples: apples, bananas, oranges, fruits, vegetables

.

Plastic #5

PP, or Polypropylene, is a tough, water resistant plastic. Products made from PP cannot be recycled, but are often durable so can be repurposed.

Examples: Bottles, lids, furniture, houseware, automobile parts

.

Plastic #6

PS, or Polystyrene, is a lightweight, weak plastic. Because of its weak structure, it cannot be recycled and often breaks apart in the natural environment, harming ocean life. Try to avoid these products if possible.

Examples: Styrofoam drinking cups, single use food containers, egg trays

.

Plastic #7

Plastic items labeled type #7 do not fall into any other the other 6 categories. Because of the diversity of materials used in these products, they cannot be recycled.

Examples: Baby bottles, fiberglass, multi-material packaging.
